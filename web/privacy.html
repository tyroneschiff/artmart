Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 8, 2026

Draw Up lets parents turn their children's drawings into AI-rendered worlds. We collect the minimum we need to make that work and we never sell your data. Below is the plain-English version of what we do and don't do.

What we collect

  • Account info. Your email address and a chosen display name. Used to sign you in and identify you in galleries you create.
  • Photos you upload. The original drawings you photograph and the AI-rendered worlds we produce. Stored privately in our hosting infrastructure (Supabase). Visible only to you unless you publish them to a gallery.
  • Galleries and pieces. The child's name you assign to a gallery, titles you give pieces, and AI-generated descriptions. Public when you publish them.
  • Engagement. Votes, comments, follows, and which pieces you open. Used to power Discover and Following feeds.
  • Payments. If you buy credits, Stripe processes the payment. We receive a confirmation and a transaction ID — never your full card number.
  • Anonymous device info. Push notification tokens (if you allow), basic crash and diagnostic data (via Apple).

What we don't collect

  • We don't collect personal information about children. The child's first name is the only child-related data we store, and it's provided by you.
  • We don't track you across other apps or websites.
  • We don't sell your data. To anyone. Ever.

Who can see what

  • Your originals (the drawings you photograph) stay private to you unless you choose to share them.
  • Pieces you publish are visible to anyone with the gallery link, and may surface on the public Discover feed.
  • Your email and engagement history are private to you.
  • Comments you post are public alongside your display name.

AI processing

To transform a drawing into a world, we send the photo to Anthropic (Claude) for description and to fal.ai (Flux Kontext) for image generation. These providers process the image to fulfill your request; they don't retain it for training. We never include identifying information about the child.

Read Aloud uses ElevenLabs to generate audio from the AI description. The same applies: it's used to render the audio, not to train models.

Deleting your account

Open the app → Profile → "Delete account." This permanently removes your account, all galleries, pieces, comments, votes, and follows. We retain anonymized order/transaction records as required for accounting and tax compliance, but they can no longer be linked to you.

Data we keep for legal reasons

Transaction records (anonymized after account deletion) are retained as long as required by applicable tax and accounting law in the US (typically 7 years).

Children's privacy (COPPA)

Draw Up is designed for parents to use on behalf of their children. The app is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 directly. Account creation is intended for parents and guardians. The child's first name, when stored, is treated as information provided by the parent and used only to label the gallery.

Your rights

You can view, export, or delete your data at any time from inside the app. If you're in a region with specific data rights (EU/UK GDPR, California CCPA), you have the right to access, correct, port, and delete your data. Email hello@drawup.ink to make a request and we'll respond within 30 days.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Email hello@drawup.ink.
